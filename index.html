History of Chivalry

Chivalry was a medieval code of conduct associated with knights in Europe during the Middle Ages. It combined martial skill with ideals of honor, loyalty, courage, and respect. Knights were expected to defend the weak, remain loyal to their lords, and uphold justice.

The concept evolved between the 11th and 15th centuries and was influenced by Christian values, feudal traditions, and courtly culture. Stories of legendary knights helped spread the ideals of chivalry across Europe.

Core Values of Chivalry

Courage

Knights were expected to face danger bravely and protect others even at great personal risk.

Honor

Honor meant living with integrity, keeping one's word, and maintaining personal dignity.

Loyalty

Loyalty to one's lord, kingdom, and companions was a central pillar of the chivalric code.

Courtesy

Respect and kindness toward others, especially women and the vulnerable, were important aspects of knightly behavior.

The Legacy of Chivalry

Although the age of knights has long passed, the ideals of chivalry continue to influence modern concepts of ethics, leadership, and personal conduct. Courage, integrity, and respect remain timeless virtues valued across cultures.

Today, the spirit of chivalry can be seen in acts of service, leadership with honor, and standing up for justice.
